## Candidate-distance audit

The strong public routes are calibration variants:

| Pair | Visible prediction RMS distance |
|---|---:|
| Public rebuild vs safe rebuild | 0.000000 ft |
| Public rebuild vs dual track | 0.010977 ft |
| Public rebuild vs G040/S12 | 0.220617 ft |
| Public rebuild vs A04 | 0.397730 ft |
| Earlier particle model vs public rebuild | 9.412087 ft |

The public rebuild and safe rebuild have the same visible output hash, but their
hidden public scores are `9.571` and `7.130`, a `2.441` RMSE gap. The result
rejects the assumption that visible output identity implies hidden execution
identity. Their dependencies, fallback paths, and hidden-test behavior must be
audited as part of the model. Correlation rounds to 1.0 for every strong pair
and is therefore a poor diversity measure; RMS delta and per-well range are
still useful, but they cannot replace lineage auditing.

Among the three visible test wells, cross-candidate uncertainty ranks
`00bbac68` first, followed by `00e12e8b` and `000d7d20`. This is a routing
signal only and must not be described as hidden-label accuracy.
