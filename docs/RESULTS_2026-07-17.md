## Additional public research findings

### Seed independence

The 30-well public audit explicitly materializes all random streams. The original versus `base + 1` check shares seven of eight seeds and has per-well RMSE correlation `0.983` with mean absolute well delta `0.612`. The zero-overlap check has correlation `0.846` and mean delta `2.166`. This makes the bookkeeping failure observable rather than only describing it.

### One coefficient, three objectives

On all 773 true boundaries, the direct-TVT trend coefficient that minimizes official pooled RMSE is `0.025046`. The macro-well optimum is `0.0196`, while the p95-well optimum is `0.0132`. Five-fold out-of-well pooled RMSE is `15.8350`. The notebook demonstrates why macro and p95 are useful diagnostics but are not substitutes for the official pooled objective.

### Typewell GR motif ambiguity

The 773 paired typewell files contain 752 byte-level unique curves, 13 duplicate hash groups, and one group of size 10. After resampling to 1-ft TVT, the median strongest distant 40-ft motif similarity is `0.8350`; `5.95%` of typewells exceed `0.90`, and `1.55%` exceed `0.95`. Strong local GR similarity therefore does not guarantee a unique TVT branch.

### Boundary geometry and score concentration

The longest 10% of wells contain `15.03%` of evaluation rows and contribute `22.83%` of anchor SSE. The highest-SSE 10% of wells contain only `11.40%` of rows but contribute `52.48%` of SSE. This supports boundary-aware validation strata and explicit largest-contributor audits.

### Prefix-only GR calibration

Across 773 visible prefixes, robust affine typewell-to-horizontal calibration has median gain `0.8504`, median offset `11.8434`, and median raw prefix correlation `0.8141`. It lowers ordinary residual RMSE on `96.12%` of wells; the median calibrated/raw RMSE ratio is `0.9348`. Robust residual sigma improves on `72.96%` of wells, and no fitted gain reaches the `[0.35, 2.5]` guard. Calibration helps the observation model but does not remove low-correlation branch ambiguity.

## One-parameter direct-TVT shrinkage

A direct TVT slope is fit on the last 30 visible rows, clipped to `[-0.1, 0.1]`, then multiplied by one global shrinkage coefficient. The coefficient is learned analytically on training wells.

- Full-fit coefficient: `0.0250460`
- Five-fold coefficients: `0.02816, 0.02213, 0.03304, 0.01571, 0.02636`
- Five-fold by-well OOF pooled RMSE: `15.8350`
- Full-fit pooled RMSE: `15.7623`

This is a real but small gain. It was not submitted because the daily budget is five and local validation already answers the question.

## Why naive multi-cut gating can mislead

Using cuts at 40%, 60%, and 80% of the visible prefix made a per-well trend calibration saturate at its maximum coefficient for most wells. Extending those locally preferred coefficients to the real suffix worsened pooled RMSE to `17.0369`.

The lesson is that more synthetic cuts are not automatically more honest. Cut positions must match the real prediction-start distribution and regime. Early-lateral dynamics can systematically overstate trend persistence at the supplied boundary.

### Multi-seed U-state particle tracker

Three full true-start replays on all 773 wells used 300 particles, eight seeds, and likelihood temperature 20. An audit caught that seed bases `20260717` and `20260718` produce consecutive eight-seed ranges with seven seeds in common, so the second run is correctly labeled an overlapping-seed check rather than an independent replication. Seed base `20270717` supplies the genuinely disjoint confirmation.

| Candidate | Seed 20260717 | Overlap 20260718 | Disjoint 20270717 |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| Anchor | 15.9099 | 15.9099 | 15.9099 |
| Particle | 14.1804 | 14.1238 | 14.5647 |
| 50/50 blend | 13.2356 | 13.1855 | 13.4192 |
| 62.5% particle blend | 13.1119 | 13.0549 | 13.3505 |
| 65% particle blend | 13.1167 | 13.0588 | 13.3662 |

The independently fitted optimal particle weights for the original and disjoint runs were `0.6253` and `0.5975`. Their joint optimum was `0.6113`; fixed 0.625 scored `13.2317` across those two runs, versus `13.2302` at the joint optimum. The five-fold out-of-well learned-weight RMSE values were `13.1544` and `13.3577`.

Across the original and disjoint runs, fixed-blend per-well RMSE correlation was `0.9003`, and the worst-decile sets overlapped on 62 of 78 wells. Fixed 0.625 p95/worst RMSE was `25.1211 / 56.0336` for the original run and `25.4364 / 70.5435` for the disjoint run, versus anchor `29.0108 / 70.6394`. The p95 improvement is stable, while the disjoint worst case shows that stochastic branch failures remain.

The first full run, its five-fold weight stability, and the overlapping check justified one leaderboard submission before the overlap issue was noticed. The genuinely disjoint post-submit audit still shows a large gain, but the record must not overstate its independence. The private notebook was executed on Kaggle before submission, its output and log were downloaded, and all 14,151 visible-test IDs and finite predictions passed the independent sample audit. Submission `54779893` scored `12.774` public RMSE. No weight variants will be submitted without a new local hypothesis.
